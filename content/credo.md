## The Three Pillars

### Think Clearly

Clarity is the foundation of effective action. In a world of noise, distraction, and misinformation, the ability to cut through complexity and see reality as it is—not as we wish it to be—is a superpower. 

Clear thinking requires:
- **Intellectual honesty**: The willingness to confront uncomfortable truths
- **Discipline**: The practice of focused attention and rigorous analysis
- **Humility**: The recognition that our perceptions are always incomplete

This principle runs through all my work, from the practical Stoicism of *The Stoic CGM* to the historical analysis of *On Proportion*. It's about seeing the world with precision and acting with intention.

### Live Deliberately

Deliberate living means aligning actions with values, not impulses. It's the practice of conscious choice over automatic reaction, of purpose over habit.

Living deliberately involves:
- **Agency**: Taking responsibility for our choices
- **Proportion**: Balancing competing priorities with wisdom
- **Commitment**: Following through on what matters most

This principle is at the heart of *Unstuck* and *A Life Made Whole*—books that help readers move from passive existence to active, intentional living.

### Love Intensely

Love, in this context, means connection with intensity. It's the recognition that relationships—with people, with ideas, with the world—are what give life meaning and power. 

Loving fully requires:
- **Intensity**: The commitment to engage fully
- **Presence**: The discipline of being fully engaged
- **Vulnerability**: The courage to connect authentically

This principle emerges in *Misaligned* and *Letters*—works that explore the human connections that shape our lives and our understanding of the world.

## The Credo in Practice

These three principles form a cycle:

1. **Think clearly** to understand what's true
2. **Live deliberately** to act on that understanding
3. **Love fully** to connect that action to meaning

Together, they create a framework for a life well-lived—a life of purpose, connection, and continuous growth.
