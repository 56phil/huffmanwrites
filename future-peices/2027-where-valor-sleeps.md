# Where Valor Sleeps

Beneath the sky of blue so wide,
Where golden light and shadows glide,
There lies a field of sacred keep—
The ground where valor lies in sleep.

Not silent stones, nor names engraved,
Could ever tell the lives they gave.
No marble cold, no gilded crest,
Could hold the weight of such a test.

They stood where duty called them forth,
From east to west, from south to north.
Through storm and fire, through smoke and dread,
They marched where lesser souls would tread.

Some fell where cannons roared so loud,
Their voices lost beneath the shroud.
Some in the hush of foreign sand,
Where distant waves kissed unknown land.

Some in the skies, so high, so free,
Now stars themselves for all to see.
Some in the depths where shadows creep,
Where only tides their vigil keep.

Yet in the dawn, when light breaks through,
And dew still clings to red and blue,
We see them stand in rows so neat—
Not gone, but waiting, strong, complete.

For freedom is not bought with gold,
Nor won by words so brave and bold.
It is the gift of those who dare
To stand between the world and despair.

So raise no wail, nor sound the knell,
But let the bugle’s echo swell.
Not for the lost, but for the light—
The torch they passed through darkest night.

And when you walk on fields so green,
Where poppies sway and sunlight gleams,
Remember well the debt we owe—
Not just to those who rest below,

But to the flame they left behind,
The love they taught, the peace they signed.
For they did not die to be mourned,
But to ensure the world was born

Anew, in hope, in strength, in grace,
A land where all might find their place.
So honor them not just with tears,
But with the work of all our years.

Live well, love deep, stand tall, stand true,
And know their sacrifice lives in you.
For every dawn, every sun’s rise,
Is theirs to claim—their sacrifice.
